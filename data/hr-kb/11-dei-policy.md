# Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Policy

**Document ID:** HR-510
**Version:** 2.0
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** Chief People Officer and Head of DEI (dei@tulpix.example)
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees, candidates, contractors, and Board members

---

## 1. Our position

Tulpix believes that diverse, equitable, and inclusive teams build better products, make better decisions, and create a workplace where every person can do meaningful work. DEI is not a side program — it is part of how we hire, manage, develop, recognize, and grow our people.

## 2. Definitions

- **Diversity** is the mix of people we have. Across protected characteristics (race, ethnicity, nationality, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, religion, disability, neurodiversity, marital and parental status, socio-economic background) and across cognitive, professional, and life experiences.
- **Equity** is fair access. It means recognizing that people start from different places and adjusting how we recruit, develop, and reward so that outcomes are not determined by background.
- **Inclusion** is the daily experience. It is the feeling of being welcome, heard, respected, and able to contribute fully.

## 3. Legal basis

We comply with EU equal treatment directives (2000/43/EC, 2000/78/EC, 2006/54/EC, 2010/41/EU, the EU Pay Transparency Directive 2023/970), and Dutch laws including:

- Algemene wet gelijke behandeling (AWGB)
- Wet gelijke behandeling van mannen en vrouwen (WGB)
- Wet gelijke behandeling op grond van handicap of chronische ziekte (WGBHCZ)
- Wet gelijke behandeling op grond van leeftijd bij de arbeid (WGBL)
- Wet gelijke behandeling van mannen en vrouwen (WGBmv)
- Wet bestuur en toezicht in pensioenfondsen (representation rules)
- Wet ingroeiquotum en streefcijfers (gender quota for top corporate roles)

## 4. Hiring

### 4.1 Inclusive job descriptions

Every job description is reviewed for:

- Gender-neutral language (using gender decoder tooling).
- Removal of unnecessary "must-have" requirements that disadvantage under-represented candidates.
- A clear statement of salary range, in line with the EU Pay Transparency Directive.
- A statement encouraging candidates from under-represented backgrounds to apply.

### 4.2 Diverse slates

For every Manager-level (M3) and above hire:

- The shortlist must include at least **two candidates from under-represented gender groups** in the relevant function.
- The hiring panel must include **at least one woman or non-binary person** and ideally one external perspective.

### 4.3 Structured interviews

Interviews use structured rubrics, with prepared questions and per-competency scoring. Unstructured "fit" interviews are not permitted as a sole basis for decisions.

### 4.4 Bias training for interviewers

Anyone interviewing candidates must complete the *Inclusive Interviewing* course (90 min) before their first panel and refresh annually.

## 5. Pay equity

See `07-compensation-and-salary.md` sections 8 and 9. Tulpix conducts:

- **Public:** Annual gender pay gap report (March).
- **Internal:** Bi-annual equal pay audit by gender and (where dataset is large enough) ethnicity, region of origin, and disability status.

Statistically unjustified gaps trigger off-cycle corrections.

## 6. Career development

### 6.1 Sponsorship and mentoring

- All employees from under-represented groups (self-identified) have access to the **Tulpix Sponsorship Program**, matching them with a senior leader who actively advocates for their advancement.
- Tulpix runs an opt-in **mentoring marketplace** for all employees.

### 6.2 Promotion calibration

Promotion decisions are calibrated by leadership panels that explicitly review demographic distributions. Disparities are surfaced and addressed.

### 6.3 Leadership development

- **Tulpix Lead** — 9-month leadership development program; at least 50% of seats reserved for women and non-binary participants.
- **Tulpix Tech Path** — engineering career growth; targeted invitations to participants from under-represented backgrounds.

## 7. Inclusive workplace

### 7.1 Accessibility

All Tulpix offices comply with the European Accessibility Act (2019/882), including:

- Step-free access, accessible toilets, accessible meeting rooms.
- Adjustable desks at every workstation.
- Hearing loops in main meeting rooms.
- Assistive technology available on request (screen readers, braille displays, ergonomic equipment).

Accessibility issues should be reported to facilities@tulpix.example or via the Accessibility Slack channel `#a11y`.

### 7.2 Neurodiversity

- Reasonable adjustments are available for ADHD, autism, dyslexia, and other neurodivergent conditions.
- Common adjustments include: written instructions, advance agendas, flexible hours, noise-cancelling headphones, dedicated quiet spaces, alternative interview formats.
- Requests are made via accommodations@tulpix.example. No medical diagnosis is required to receive support; the focus is on what helps you do your work well.

### 7.3 Religion and belief

- Quiet rooms (multi-faith) are available at every office.
- Up to 2 paid days per year may substitute for public holidays in observance of religious or cultural events not in the Tulpix calendar (see `01-leave-and-vacation-policy.md` section 7).
- Reasonable scheduling adjustments around prayer times and religious fasts (e.g., Ramadan) are supported.

### 7.4 LGBTQ+ inclusion

- Tulpix is a signatory of the *Workplace Pride* declaration.
- Same-sex partners are equal in all benefits, leave, and emergency contacts.
- Trans and non-binary employees may update name and pronouns in all systems without legal documentation. Tulpix supports the legal and medical journey, including paid leave for gender-affirming procedures.

## 9. Employee resource groups (ERGs)

Tulpix supports ERGs for community building and as advisory groups to leadership. Current ERGs:

- **Women @ Tulpix**
- **Pride @ Tulpix**
- **Black @ Tulpix**
- **APIDA @ Tulpix** (Asian, Pacific Islander, and Desi American)
- **Latinx @ Tulpix**
- **Disability & Neurodiversity Alliance**
- **Parents @ Tulpix**
- **Newcomers** (international hires and relocators)

Each ERG receives an annual budget of €5,000–€15,000 depending on size, plus paid leadership time (4 hours/month for chairs).

## 11. Reporting concerns

Concerns about discrimination, harassment, or exclusion are handled through `12-grievance-and-whistleblower.md`. Independent escalation routes:

- **College voor de Rechten van de Mens** (Netherlands Institute for Human Rights) — independent body that can rule on discrimination cases.
- **Ondernemingsraad (Works Council)** — or@tulpix.example.
