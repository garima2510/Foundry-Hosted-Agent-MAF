# Grievance & Whistleblower Procedure

**Document ID:** HR-520
**Version:** 3.0
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** Chief People Officer, Chief Compliance Officer
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees, contractors, interns, agency workers, former employees (within 2 years of leaving), candidates, and Board members

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure describes how to raise concerns about misconduct, breaches of policy or law, or unfair treatment at Tulpix, and how Tulpix handles them. It is designed to encourage early, honest reporting and to protect those who speak up.

This procedure complies with:

- The EU Whistleblowing Directive (2019/1937).
- The Dutch Wet bescherming klokkenluiders (Whistleblower Protection Act) effective 18 February 2023.
- The Wet op de Ondernemingsraden (Works Council Act).

## 2. Scope

You may use this procedure to raise concerns about, for example:

- Discrimination, harassment, bullying, or retaliation
- Breach of laws or regulations (privacy, competition, sanctions, financial reporting, environmental, product safety)
- Fraud, theft, embezzlement, or accounting irregularities
- Bribery, corruption, conflict of interest, or improper gifts
- Breach of internal policies (Code of Conduct, IT security, data protection)
- Unfair pay or progression decisions
- Workplace safety risks
- Wrongdoing concerning third parties (customers, suppliers, partners) where Tulpix may be implicated

Personal grievances unrelated to wrongdoing (e.g., disagreement with a performance rating) follow the **internal grievance** route in section 4.

## 3. Channels

You may raise a concern through any of these channels. There is no requirement to use one before another.

### 3.1 Direct route (informal first attempt — optional)

If safe and appropriate, raise the matter informally with the person involved or with your manager. Many issues are resolved this way.

### 3.2 HR / Manager (formal internal)

- Your manager
- Your skip-level manager
- Your HR Business Partner
- people@tulpix.example

### 3.4 Speak Up — anonymous whistleblower hotline

Operated by an independent provider (Whistleblower Direct):

- Web: https://speak-up.tulpix.example
- Email: speak-up@tulpix.example
- Phone (24/7, multilingual): +31 20 555 0199

Reports can be **fully anonymous**. The provider assigns a case number; you may continue to communicate via the platform without revealing your identity.

### 3.5 Works Council (Ondernemingsraad)

For concerns about company-wide practices, especially affecting groups of employees: or@tulpix.example.

### 3.6 External authorities

You may report directly to a competent external authority. Common ones:

- **Huis voor Klokkenluiders** (Netherlands House for Whistleblowers) — for breaches of Dutch law.
- **Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens** (Dutch DPA) — privacy and GDPR.
- **ACM** (Authority for Consumers and Markets) — competition, consumer protection.
- **AFM** / **DNB** — financial misconduct.
- **NLA / Inspectie SZW** — labor and OSH.
- **EU Commission and EU agencies** — for breaches of EU law.

You retain whistleblower protection when reporting to an authority.

### 3.7 Public disclosure

Public disclosure (e.g., to media) retains whistleblower protection only in limited situations under the directive — for example, where internal and external reporting are inadequate, or there is imminent danger to public interest. Take legal advice via legal@tulpix.example or an independent lawyer before considering this route.

## 4. Internal grievance (personal disputes)

If your concern is a **personal disagreement** (e.g., with a manager's decision on leave, pay, performance review, allocation):

1. **Step 1 — Informal discussion** with the person involved.
2. **Step 2 — Manager** (or skip-level if the manager is the subject) within 30 days of the issue.
3. **Step 3 — Formal grievance** to your HR Business Partner. Decision within 30 days.
4. **Step 4 — Appeal** to the Chief People Officer. Decision within 30 days.
5. **Step 5 — External:** Mediation, the Cantonal Court (Kantonrechter), or other competent forum.

Where the dispute concerns possible discrimination, you may use the whistleblower procedure or both procedures in parallel.

## 5. How a whistleblower report is handled

### 5.1 Acknowledgement

You receive an acknowledgement **within 7 days** (statutory requirement under the Wet bescherming klokkenluiders).

### 5.2 Initial review

A **case officer** is assigned. The case officer is independent of the parties involved. For very serious or sensitive matters, an external investigator (e.g., Bureau Vertrouwenswerk, an external law firm) is engaged.

The initial review determines:

- Whether the matter falls within scope.
- Whether interim safety measures are needed (e.g., temporary suspension or work separation).
- The investigation plan.

### 5.3 Investigation

- Documentary review, interviews, and (where relevant) forensic analysis.
- Both the reporter and the subject are heard, except where confidentiality requires staged disclosure.
- Evidence is preserved in line with the Tulpix retention schedule and legal hold.

### 5.4 Feedback

You receive substantive feedback **no later than 3 months** from the acknowledgement (statutory). Where the investigation is complex, you receive interim updates every 30 days.

Findings communicated typically include:

- Whether the concern was substantiated.
- The actions Tulpix is taking (without breaching the privacy rights of others).

### 5.5 Outcomes

Possible outcomes include:

- No wrongdoing found.
- Process improvements, training, or remediation.
- Disciplinary action up to and including dismissal of the wrongdoer.
- Civil claim, criminal report, or regulatory notification.
- Restorative actions (apology, reinstatement, back-pay, accommodations).

## 6. Non-retaliation

Tulpix prohibits any retaliation against a person who, in good faith, raises a concern, supports a report, or participates in an investigation. Retaliation includes:

- Dismissal, demotion, suspension, or denial of training, promotion, or pay increase.
- Negative performance review, hostile workplace, ostracism.
- Disciplinary measures, transfers, change of duties or location.
- Coercion, intimidation, harassment.
- Damage to reputation.
- Failure to renew or early termination of a contract.
- Blacklisting.

Retaliation is itself a serious breach of this Code and is investigated separately. Retaliation may be presumed under the Wet bescherming klokkenluiders, with the **burden of proof shifted to Tulpix** to show otherwise.

## 7. Confidentiality

The identity of the reporter, the subject, and any third parties involved is kept confidential to the extent possible. Disclosure is limited to those who need to know to investigate or act. The reporter's identity is not shared with the subject without consent, except where required by law (e.g., in court proceedings).

## 8. Bad faith reports

Reports made knowingly false or in bad faith are not protected and may themselves be subject to disciplinary action. Honest concerns that turn out to be unfounded **remain protected**.

## 9. Anonymous reports

Anonymous reports are accepted and investigated. They may be harder to investigate due to the inability to follow up. Use the Speak Up platform to maintain a two-way conversation while remaining anonymous.

## 10. Records

Tulpix maintains a confidential register of reports and outcomes. Records are kept for **5 years** after closure (or longer if required for litigation). Personal data is processed in line with Tulpix's privacy notice for whistleblower processing, available on the intranet.

## 11. Annual review

The Chief People Officer and Chief Compliance Officer publish an annual summary of:

- Number of reports by category
- Outcomes
- Process improvements

The summary is shared with the Works Council and (in anonymized form) with all employees.
