# Remote & Hybrid Work Policy

**Document ID:** HR-310
**Version:** 2.5
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees in the EU

---

## 1. Our work model

Tulpix operates a **hybrid-first** work model. Most roles can be performed from home, from a Tulpix office, or in a combination of both. The default expectation is:

- **Minimum 2 days per week from a Tulpix office** (or designated co-working location for fully distributed teams).
- **Up to 3 days per week from home** within the contracted country.
- **Team anchor day:** at least one fixed in-office day per week, set by the team lead, where the whole team is co-located.

Roles that require physical presence (e.g., facilities, IT lab, hardware engineering) follow team-specific schedules agreed with the line manager.

## 2. Eligibility

All employees who have completed their **probationary period** (typically 1 or 2 months) are eligible for hybrid working. New hires work primarily from the office during onboarding to facilitate integration.

Employees may request a non-standard pattern under the Wet flexibel werken — see `05-working-hours-policy.md` section 7.

## 3. Working from another EU country (workation)

### 3.1 Up to 30 calendar days per year

Employees may work from any EU member state for up to **30 calendar days per rolling 12-month period**, subject to:

- **2 weeks advance notice** to the line manager.
- Maintaining standard working hours in their home country's time zone (or as agreed with manager and team).
- Having reliable internet (minimum 25 Mbps down / 5 Mbps up) and a quiet workspace.
- A maximum of **14 consecutive days** in any single trip.

The 30-day limit prevents triggering tax residency or social security implications under the EU Framework Agreement on telework (effective 1 July 2023).

### 3.2 More than 30 days

Working abroad for longer than 30 days/year requires a formal review by HR, payroll, and tax. Approvals are granted case-by-case and may require:

- A1 certificate (social security)
- Local tax registration
- Adjusted contract terms

Submit requests via Workday at least **8 weeks** in advance.

### 3.3 Working outside the EU

Working from outside the EU is generally not permitted without a formal international assignment. Short trips (e.g., visiting family) where you do not work are unrestricted.

## 4. Home office requirements

### 4.1 Workspace

Your home workspace must:

- Be private enough to handle confidential information (no shared screens visible to others).
- Allow ergonomic posture for the duration of working hours.
- Have stable internet (see section 3.1).
- Comply with the Arbo (occupational health) requirements covered in `17-health-and-safety.md`.

### 4.2 Equipment

Tulpix provides:

- Laptop (refreshed every 3 years)
- External monitor (one 27" 4K display, additional on request with manager approval)
- Keyboard, mouse, headset
- Webcam (if not built into the laptop)

Employees may select from the standard hardware catalog on the intranet. Custom hardware requests require IT and manager approval.

### 4.3 Home office allowance

Tulpix pays a tax-free home office allowance of **€2.40 per day worked from home** (in line with the Dutch werkkostenregeling 2026 vrije ruimte). This is automatically calculated based on your declared working pattern in Workday.

A one-time **€500 home office setup allowance** is paid to new hires for purchasing a desk, chair, lighting, etc. Receipts are not required (covered under the WKR free space).

### 4.4 Connectivity

Employees may submit a monthly internet expense of up to **€30** via the expense system. Mobile phones are provided for roles requiring frequent travel or on-call duty (see Travel & Expense policy `13-travel-and-expense.md`).

## 5. Communication and collaboration norms

### 5.1 Asynchronous-first

We prefer asynchronous communication for most work to support different schedules and time zones. Meetings should be reserved for:

- Decisions requiring discussion
- Brainstorming and design sessions
- 1:1s and team rituals
- Onboarding and feedback

### 5.2 Meeting hygiene

- Default meeting length is **25 or 50 minutes**, leaving 5–10 minutes for transitions.
- All recurring meetings must have a written purpose and agenda. Annual review: cancel if not delivering value.
- **No-meeting Wednesdays** for engineering and product teams.
- Calendar boundaries (e.g., "no meetings before 09:30" or "blocked focus time") are respected.

### 5.3 Tools

Standard tools:
- **Slack** — real-time chat (response expectation: 4 hours during working hours).
- **Email** — external + formal communication (response: 1 working day).
- **Confluence** — documentation.
- **Jira** — work tracking.
- **Google Meet** — video calls.

## 6. Working from the office

Tulpix offices offer:

- Open desks (book via Robin)
- Quiet rooms and phone booths
- Meeting rooms (book via calendar)
- Free lunch on anchor days at HQ Amsterdam, Berlin, Paris (catered, vegetarian default with options)
- Coffee, tea, fruit
- Showers and bike storage at Amsterdam, Berlin, Paris
- Mothers' rooms / lactation rooms at all offices

## 7. Time zone management

For cross-country teams, the following overlap minimums apply:

- Teams spanning **CET ± 1 hour** (NL, DE, FR, ES, IE): at least **4 hours** of daily overlap.
- Distributed individuals not on a "team": at least **2 hours** of overlap with their primary collaborators.

## 8. Manager responsibilities

Managers of hybrid/remote employees must:

- Set clear, measurable expectations and outcomes.
- Run regular 1:1s (at least every 2 weeks).
- Recognize work fairly across in-office and remote contributions.
- Not require off-hours availability beyond contractual on-call.
- Report concerns about engagement or wellbeing to HR early.

## 9. Termination of arrangement

Tulpix may, with **3 months' notice**, change the hybrid arrangement for an individual or team if:

- The role's requirements change.
- Performance issues are identified that the company doctor or HR believes are linked to remote work.
- New compliance requirements arise.

In all cases the change must be reasonable and proportionate, and the employee retains the right to formally object via the grievance procedure.

## 10. Subsidiary specifics

- **Germany:** Mobile work is governed by the Mobile Arbeit policy. Working from home requires risk assessment under ArbStättV.
- **France:** Télétravail requires a charter or amendment to the contract. Tickets-restaurant apply on home-office days.
- **Ireland:** Right to Request Remote Working Code of Practice 2024 applies. Refusals must follow the statutory process.
