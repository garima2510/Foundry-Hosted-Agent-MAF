# 30% Ruling for Foreign Employees (NL)

**Document ID:** HR-420
**Version:** 2.1
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** Total Rewards (rewards@tulpix.example) and Global Mobility (mobility@tulpix.example)
**Applies to:** Employees recruited from abroad to work in the Netherlands at Tulpix B.V.

---

## 1. What is the 30% ruling?

The Dutch 30% ruling (30%-regeling) is a tax facility that allows qualifying employees recruited from abroad to receive **up to 30% of their gross salary as a tax-free allowance**, intended to compensate for the additional costs of working in a foreign country (extraterritorial costs).

The ruling is set out in Article 31a of the Wet op de loonbelasting 1964 and is administered by the Belastingdienst.

## 2. 2024 reform and 2026 status

Under reforms enacted by the Belastingplan 2024 and adjusted by Belastingplan 2025:

- The previous **stepped reduction** (30% / 20% / 10% over 5 years) was **withdrawn**.
- A revised **flat percentage** is in effect:
  - **27% tax-free allowance** for new applications from **1 January 2027**.
  - **30% tax-free allowance** continues to apply for employees who **first used the ruling before 1 January 2024** (transitional rule, valid for the original term).
  - For applications between **1 January 2024 and 31 December 2026**, the rate is **30% for the full term**.
- The **maximum term remains 5 years**.
- The **salary norm** is increased annually with inflation (see section 3.2).
- The **Balkenende-norm** (cap on the salary base) has been **abolished from 1 January 2025** — the 30% ruling now applies to the full salary, regardless of how high.
- The option to be treated as a **partial non-resident taxpayer** (partieel buitenlands belastingplichtige) for Box 2 and Box 3 income was **abolished from 1 January 2025**.

## 3. Eligibility requirements

To qualify, all of the following must be true:

### 3.1 Recruited from abroad

The employee must have been **recruited from abroad** or **transferred from a foreign group entity** to a Dutch employer. The employee must not have lived within **150 km of the Dutch border for at least 16 of the 24 months** before the start of employment.

### 3.2 Specific expertise — salary norm

The employee must have **specific expertise** that is scarce in the Dutch labor market. This is presumed if the gross taxable annual salary (excluding the 30% allowance) exceeds the annual norm:

- **2026 standard norm:** **€46,660** taxable salary.
- **Reduced norm for masters under 30:** **€35,468**.
- **Scientific researchers and certain medical specialists:** no salary norm applies.

### 3.3 Valid 30% ruling decision

The Belastingdienst must issue a 30% ruling decision (beschikking). Tulpix's mobility partner submits the application on behalf of new hires within **4 months** of the start date — applications submitted within this window apply retroactively to the start date.

## 4. Benefits to the employee

### 4.1 Tax-free allowance

Up to 30% of gross salary is reclassified as a tax-free allowance, reducing taxable income and therefore income tax (Box 1).

Example: An employee with a gross annual package of €100,000 may receive €70,000 as taxable salary and €30,000 as tax-free allowance — provided the resulting taxable salary still exceeds the salary norm.

### 4.2 Driver's license exchange

30% ruling holders (and their family members) may exchange their foreign driver's license for a Dutch one without retaking exams.

### 4.3 (Until 31 Dec 2024) Partial non-resident treatment

Employees with a 30% ruling could elect partial non-resident status, exempting them from Dutch tax on Box 2 (substantial interest) and Box 3 (savings/investments). **This option no longer applies from 1 January 2025**, except for transitional cases (employees with a ruling effective on or before 31 December 2023, until end-2026).

## 5. How Tulpix processes the 30% ruling

### 5.1 Application

For eligible new hires, Tulpix's mobility partner (Bridgehead Tax B.V.) prepares and submits the joint application to the Belastingdienst. Tulpix covers the application fee.

### 5.2 Salary structure

Once the ruling is granted:

- Tulpix splits the agreed gross package into:
  - **Taxable salary** (at least the salary norm).
  - **Tax-free 30% allowance** (up to the maximum permitted).
- The split is shown on each payslip.
- The total cost-to-employer is unchanged. The employee receives more net pay.

### 5.3 Salary review impact

When salary is reviewed, the gross package is adjusted as a whole. The split between taxable and tax-free is then re-calculated to ensure the salary norm is met.

## 6. Loss of eligibility

The 30% ruling can be **withdrawn or partly recalculated** if:

- Taxable salary falls below the salary norm in any calendar year (e.g., due to extended unpaid leave, parental leave, or salary reduction).
- The employee leaves Tulpix (the ruling is employer-specific). A new employer in the Netherlands within **3 months** can take over the remaining term, subject to a new application.
- The employee no longer qualifies under the recruited-from-abroad criterion (rare, e.g., return after multi-year absence).

## 7. Term and end

- The ruling has a maximum term of **5 years (60 months)**, reduced by previous periods of stay or work in the Netherlands within the past 25 years.
- Tulpix tracks ruling end dates centrally. **6 months before the end**, employees receive a notification and an offer to discuss the impact on net pay.

## 9. Frequently asked

**Q: Does the 30% allowance count for pension contributions?**
A: No. Pension contributions are calculated on the **taxable** salary base only. Employees may opt to add an extra contribution under the bijspaarmodule.

**Q: Does the 30% allowance count for mortgage applications?**
A: Most Dutch lenders accept the full gross package (including the 30% allowance) for mortgage assessment, but treat it conservatively. Practice varies — discuss with your mortgage adviser.

**Q: What happens if I take parental leave?**
A: The unpaid portion of parental leave reduces taxable salary. If this drops below the norm in a calendar year, the ruling cannot be applied for that year. Plan with HR ahead of long leaves.

**Q: Does the 30% ruling apply to bonuses, RSUs, and overtime?**
A: Yes — the 30% can be applied to all wage components from current employment, provided the conditions are met. Stock-based compensation receives 30% allocation in the year of vesting (subject to ruling still being valid).

**Q: I previously worked in the Netherlands. Can I still get the 30% ruling?**
A: Possibly. Any prior stay or work in NL within the past 25 years reduces your maximum 5-year term. Bridgehead will check your eligibility during onboarding.
