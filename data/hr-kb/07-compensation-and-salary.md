# Compensation & Salary Policy

**Document ID:** HR-400
**Version:** 4.0
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** Total Rewards team (rewards@tulpix.example)
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees in the EU

---

## 1. Compensation philosophy

Tulpix aims to pay **competitively**, **transparently**, and **fairly**:

- **Competitive:** Salaries are benchmarked annually against the **Mercer EU Tech Compensation Survey** and **Radford Global Technology Survey**. Targets are set at the **65th percentile** of the relevant local market for the role and level.
- **Transparent:** All employees can see their level, salary band, and the band's midpoint via Workday. Pay ranges for open roles are published on the careers site, in line with the EU Pay Transparency Directive (2023/970).
- **Fair:** Pay decisions are based on role, level, performance, and location — never on gender, race, age, or other protected characteristics.

## 2. Job architecture

Roles are mapped to a global architecture with two career tracks:

| Level | Individual Contributor (IC) | People Manager (M) |
|-------|------------------------------|---------------------|
| L1 | Junior | — |
| L2 | Mid | — |
| L3 | Senior | M3 — Team Lead |
| L4 | Staff | M4 — Manager |
| L5 | Senior Staff | M5 — Senior Manager |
| L6 | Principal | M6 — Director |
| L7 | Distinguished | M7 — Senior Director |
| L8 | Fellow | M8 — VP |
| L9 | — | M9 — SVP / C-suite |

Movement between IC and M tracks is supported and not considered promotion or demotion.

## 3. Salary bands

Each (level, country) combination has a salary band with **min / mid / max**. Examples (Netherlands, gross annual, indicative for 2026):

| Level | Min | Mid | Max |
|-------|------|------|------|
| L1 | €38,000 | €44,000 | €50,000 |
| L2 | €50,000 | €60,000 | €70,000 |
| L3 | €70,000 | €82,000 | €94,000 |
| L4 | €92,000 | €108,000 | €124,000 |
| L5 | €118,000 | €138,000 | €158,000 |
| L6 | €148,000 | €172,000 | €196,000 |

Local cost-of-labor adjustments apply per country. Country differentials (relative to NL):

- Germany: +5%
- France (Paris): +0%
- Spain (Madrid): −15%
- Ireland (Dublin): +10%

Salary bands are reviewed and republished every January.

## 4. Annual review cycle

### 4.1 Timing

- **Performance review:** December–January
- **Calibration:** February
- **Compensation decisions:** March
- **New salaries effective:** 1 April

### 4.2 Eligibility

Employees who joined before **1 October** of the previous year are eligible for the full annual review. Those who joined later receive a pro-rata review or are reviewed in the next cycle, whichever is more favorable.

### 4.3 Components

The annual review may include:

1. **Merit increase:** Based on performance and position in band. Typical range 0–8% in 2026.
2. **Market adjustment:** If market data shows a position-in-band gap.
3. **Equity refresh:** RSU grant for Levels 4 and above (vesting over 4 years, 1-year cliff).
4. **Promotion:** Move to next level, with corresponding salary band. Typically 8–15% increase plus refresh equity.

Decisions are communicated in writing by **15 March** with effective date **1 April**.

## 5. Bonus

### 5.1 Annual variable pay (AVP)

All non-sales roles are eligible for an annual bonus, expressed as a target percentage of base salary:

| Level | Target AVP |
|-------|------------|
| L1–L2 | 8% |
| L3 | 10% |
| L4 | 12% |
| L5 | 15% |
| L6+ | 20%+ |

Payout is based on:

- **70% company performance** (revenue, ARR growth, profitability) — set by the Board.
- **30% individual performance** (manager rating from review).

Payout range: 0% to 200% of target. Paid in **April** along with the new salary.

### 5.2 Sales commission

Sales roles have a separate commission plan documented in the annual Sales Compensation Plan, distributed by the Sales Operations team.

### 5.3 Pro-ration

New joiners receive a pro-rated bonus based on time worked in the bonus year. Leavers (resignations) before payout typically forfeit the bonus, except where local law (e.g., Germany — Treuepflicht, France — usage) provides otherwise.

## 6. 13th month / holiday allowance

**Netherlands:** Holiday allowance (vakantiegeld) of **8% of gross annual salary** is paid in May, in line with the Wet Minimumloon en Vakantiebijslag.

**Germany / France / Spain:** Where collective agreements apply, 13th-month or 14th-month pay may apply. Refer to the local addendum.

**Ireland:** No statutory 13th month; full annual salary is paid in 12 monthly installments.

## 7. Payroll

- Monthly salary is paid on the **25th of the month** (or the prior business day if the 25th is a weekend or holiday).
- Payslips are available in Workday by the morning of payday.
- The annual statement (jaaropgaaf) is published in Workday by **1 March** of the following year.
- Payroll questions: payroll@tulpix.example. Response within 2 business days.

## 8. EU Pay Transparency Directive

Effective from 7 June 2026, Tulpix complies with the directive's requirements:

- Job postings include salary range and any objective criteria for progression.
- Candidates are not asked about salary history.
- Employees may request, **once per year**, anonymized information on average pay levels (broken down by gender) for employees doing the same or equivalent work.
- A gender pay gap report is published annually in March (when the workforce exceeds 100 employees, in line with phased rollout).
- Pay gaps of **5% or more** that cannot be objectively justified trigger a joint pay assessment with the Works Council.

## 9. Equal pay audits

Independent of the directive, Tulpix conducts an internal equal pay audit twice a year, comparing pay across gender for employees in the same level and country. Statistically significant gaps are corrected through targeted adjustments outside the annual review cycle.

## 10. Off-cycle adjustments

Off-cycle salary adjustments are reserved for:

- Promotions
- Internal moves to a higher band
- Counter-offers (rare and discretionary)
- Equal pay corrections
- Significant scope changes mid-year

Off-cycle adjustments require approval from the manager, skip-level, and the Total Rewards team.

## 11. Reference benchmarks (used internally)
