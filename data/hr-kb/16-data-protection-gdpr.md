# Data Protection & GDPR Policy (Employee Personal Data)

**Document ID:** HR-800
**Version:** 5.0
**Effective date:** 1 February 2026
**Owner:** Data Protection Officer (dpo@tulpix.example)
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees, contractors, interns, and applicants in the EU/EEA

---

## 1. Purpose and scope

This policy explains how Tulpix B.V. processes **personal data of employees, applicants, and contractors** under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, "GDPR") and the Dutch implementation act (Uitvoeringswet AVG, "UAVG").

It covers the entire employee lifecycle:

- Recruitment and pre-employment screening
- Onboarding and contract administration
- Payroll, benefits, and tax
- Performance management and learning
- Health, safety, and sickness administration
- Workplace monitoring (laptops, badges, CCTV)
- Internal investigations and grievances
- Offboarding and post-employment retention

For data of customers, partners, and website visitors, see the separate **Customer Privacy Notice** (LEG-100).

## 3. Lawful bases for processing employee data

We rely on the following Article 6 GDPR lawful bases:

| Basis | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| **Contract** (6(1)(b)) | Paying salary, providing benefits, managing performance |
| **Legal obligation** (6(1)(c)) | Tax filings, social security, identity checks (Wet op de identificatieplicht), accident reporting |
| **Legitimate interest** (6(1)(f)) | IT security monitoring, fraud prevention, business continuity |
| **Consent** (6(1)(a)) | Optional benefits, photo on intranet, employee testimonials |

We **do not rely on consent** for anything that is a condition of employment, because consent freely given by an employee is generally not valid (recital 43 GDPR; AP guidance 2024).

For special categories (health, union membership, biometric data), we rely on Article 9(2)(b) (employment law) or 9(2)(h) (occupational medicine via the company doctor).

## 4. What we collect

### 4.1 Always collected

- Identification: full name, date of birth, BSN/Steuer-ID/PPS, ID document copy, nationality.
- Contact: home address, personal email, personal phone, emergency contact.
- Employment: contract terms, role, department, manager, work location.
- Payroll: IBAN, tax classification, pension fund.
- Performance: goals, ratings, 1:1 notes (in HRIS only), promotion history.
- Time: vacation balance, sickness days (no diagnosis), working hours.

### 4.2 Conditionally collected

- Visa and work-permit data (non-EU/EEA nationals).
- Driving licence (only roles requiring driving).
- Bank statements (only for expense reimbursement disputes).
- DBS-equivalent background check (only for finance, security, and legal roles, with explicit notice).

### 4.3 Never collected

- Religious or philosophical beliefs (except where required for chaplaincy or holiday accommodation, with consent).
- Sexual orientation, except in fully voluntary, anonymized DEI surveys.
- Political opinions.
- Trade union membership (we are notified by the union, not by you).
- Genetic data.
- Criminal records, except where required by law and approved by the DPO.

## 5. Retention periods

| Data | Retention | Legal basis |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Application materials of unsuccessful candidates | 4 weeks (extended to 1 year with consent) | AP guidance |
| Employment contract & amendments | 7 years after end of employment | Dutch tax law (Algemene wet inzake rijksbelastingen, art. 52) |
| Payroll records | 7 years after end of employment | Tax law |
| ID document copy | 5 years after end of employment | Wet op de loonbelasting |
| Performance reviews | 2 years after end of employment | Legitimate interest |
| Sickness data (HR view, no diagnosis) | 2 years after recovery or end of employment | UAVG art. 30 |
| Sickness data (company doctor) | 20 years (medical record) | Wet op de geneeskundige behandelingsovereenkomst |
| CCTV footage | 4 weeks | AP guidance |
| Email and Slack messages | Per IT retention policy (default 5 years) | Legitimate interest |
| Disciplinary records | 5 years after closure | Legitimate interest |

After retention expires, data is **deleted or irreversibly anonymized** within 90 days.

## 6. Workplace monitoring

Monitoring is governed by GDPR, the Dutch Works Council Act (WOR art. 27), and AP guidance on workplace monitoring (2023).

### 6.1 What we monitor

- **Endpoint security** (CrowdStrike, Microsoft Defender) on company laptops, 24/7. Logs retained 90 days.
- **Email & Slack** for security incidents (DLP), not for content review. Manager access requires DPO + Works Council approval.
- **Badge access logs** for office entry. Retained 60 days.
- **CCTV** at office entrances and server rooms only. Retained 4 weeks. Signs are clearly displayed.
- **GitHub & code repositories** — commits and reviews are visible by design; this is not "monitoring."

### 6.2 What we do not do

- Keystroke logging.
- Webcam monitoring.
- Productivity/activity scoring (no Time Doctor, Hubstaff, etc.).
- Reading personal email or messages on personal devices.
- GPS tracking of company cars (except for fleet safety alerts; aggregated, not individual).

### 6.3 Investigations

A targeted review of an individual's digital activity requires:

1. A documented suspicion of policy violation or legal breach.
2. Written approval from the DPO and the Head of People.
3. Notification to the Works Council within 48 hours (anonymized).
4. Notification to the employee, unless this would prejudice the investigation. In that case, notification within 4 weeks of conclusion.

## 7. Your rights as a data subject

Under GDPR Articles 15–22, you have the right to:

| Right | What it means | How to exercise |
|-------|---------------|------------------|
| Access (Art. 15) | Get a copy of your data | Submit a DSAR via the privacy portal |
| Rectification (Art. 16) | Correct inaccurate data | Update in HRIS or contact People Ops |
| Erasure (Art. 17) | Have data deleted (subject to retention obligations) | DSAR via privacy portal |
| Restriction (Art. 18) | Pause processing while a dispute is resolved | DSAR via privacy portal |
| Portability (Art. 20) | Receive data in a machine-readable format | DSAR via privacy portal |
| Objection (Art. 21) | Object to processing based on legitimate interest | Email dpo@tulpix.example |
| No automated decisions (Art. 22) | We do not make solely automated employment decisions | N/A |

We respond to requests **within 1 month** (extendable by 2 months for complex requests, with notice).

You can lodge a complaint with:

- **Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens** (NL): autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl
- **Bundesbeauftragte für den Datenschutz** (DE)
- **CNIL** (FR)
- **AEPD** (ES)
- **Data Protection Commission** (IE)

## 8. Sharing and transfers

### 8.1 Internal sharing

- HRIS data is accessed only by HR, your manager, and your manager's manager. Salary is masked from skip-level managers.
- Performance reviews are visible to you, your manager, and HR.

### 8.2 External recipients

| Recipient | Data shared | Lawful basis |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| ABN AMRO (payroll bank) | Salary, IBAN | Contract |
| Belastingdienst (NL tax) | Payroll, BSN | Legal obligation |
| UWV | Sickness, parental leave claims | Legal obligation |
| Pension fund (PFZW / local equivalents) | Salary, contributions | Legal obligation |
| Health & safety service (Arbo) | Sickness data (medical) | Legal obligation |
| Background-check provider (Validata) | Limited, with consent | Legitimate interest |
| HRIS vendor (HiBob, EU hosting) | All employee data | Contract (Processor) |

### 8.3 International transfers

- All HR systems are hosted in the EU/EEA.
- Where US-based vendors are unavoidable (e.g., AWS for some logging), we rely on **Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914)** and the **EU-US Data Privacy Framework**, with documented transfer impact assessments.

## 9. Data breaches

If you suspect a data breach (lost laptop, misdirected email containing personal data, phishing victim), report it **within 1 hour** to:

- security@tulpix.example (24/7)
- dpo@tulpix.example

The DPO assesses whether the breach must be notified to the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens within **72 hours** (Art. 33 GDPR), and whether affected individuals must be informed (Art. 34).

Failure to report a known breach is a serious disciplinary matter.

## 10. AI and automated decisions

Tulpix does not make **solely automated decisions** about hiring, promotion, performance ratings, or termination.

- AI tools (e.g., CV parsing, interview scheduling assistants) are **decision-supporting**, not decision-making. A human reviews every outcome.
- Employees may request a human review of any decision they believe was AI-influenced.
- New AI tools that process employee data require a **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** and Works Council consultation before deployment.

## 11. Training and awareness

- All employees complete mandatory annual GDPR training (45 min, in HRIS).
- Managers complete an additional 30-min module on team data handling.
- Engineers handling production employee data complete a privacy-engineering module.

Non-completion within 30 days of due date results in HRIS access being suspended until completed.
