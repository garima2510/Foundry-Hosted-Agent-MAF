# Termination, Notice & Offboarding Policy

**Document ID:** HR-900
**Version:** 4.3
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture (people@tulpix.example) and Legal (legal@tulpix.example)
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees in the Netherlands and EU subsidiaries

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy describes how employment at Tulpix ends — whether by the employee, by Tulpix, or by mutual consent — and what each party's rights and obligations are. The Netherlands has one of the most employee-protective dismissal regimes in Europe, and this document reflects that.

## 2. Ways employment can end

| Route | Initiator | NL legal basis | Typical use |
|-------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| Resignation (opzegging) | Employee | BW 7:670 et seq. | Employee leaves voluntarily |
| Mutual termination (vaststellingsovereenkomst, VSO) | Both | BW 7:900 | Negotiated, most common dismissal route |
| Dismissal via UWV | Employer | BW 7:671a, 7:669(3)(a)(b) | Economic / long-term sickness |
| Dismissal via Kantonrechter | Employer | BW 7:671b | Personal reasons (performance, conduct, broken relationship) |
| Summary dismissal (ontslag op staande voet) | Employer | BW 7:677, 7:678 | Urgent and grave cause; rare |
| End of fixed-term contract (van rechtswege) | Automatic | BW 7:667 | Contract end date reached |
| Probation termination | Either | BW 7:676 | During probation period |
| Retirement | Employee | Pension regulations | Reaching state pension age (AOW) |

In other EU countries, local law applies. This document focuses on the Netherlands and flags major differences elsewhere.

## 3. Notice periods (Netherlands)

### 3.1 Statutory minimum

Per BW 7:672:

| Length of service | Employer notice | Employee notice |
|-------------------|------------------|------------------|
| <5 years | 1 month | 1 month |
| 5–9 years | 2 months | 1 month |
| 10–14 years | 3 months | 1 month |
| ≥15 years | 4 months | 1 month |

Notice runs **from the end of the calendar month**. Example: notice given on 12 March takes effect from 1 April.

### 3.2 Tulpix contractual notice

Most Tulpix employment contracts agree:

- **Employee notice: 1 month** (statutory minimum).
- **Employer notice: as per statute, with a 1-month bonus** (i.e., we add 1 month to whatever the law requires) for indefinite contracts of >2 years' tenure.

If your contract says something different, the contract prevails as long as employer notice ≥ employee notice and ≥ statutory minimum.

### 3.3 Other countries (summary)

| Country | Typical notice (employer → employee with 5 yrs tenure) |
|---------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Germany | 2 months to month-end (BGB §622) |
| France | 1–3 months depending on category and CCN |
| Spain | 15 days (objective dismissal) + severance |
| Ireland | 4 weeks (Minimum Notice and Terms of Employment Act 1973) |

## 4. Resignation (employee-initiated)

To resign:

1. Inform your manager in a 1:1.
2. Submit written resignation by email to your manager and to people@tulpix.example. Include your last working day.
3. People Ops confirms the calculation of your final day, given statutory month-end rules.
4. Hand over responsibilities per the offboarding checklist (section 9).

**Withdrawing a resignation** is possible only with Tulpix's agreement.

## 5. Mutual termination (VSO)

Most negotiated departures use a **vaststellingsovereenkomst**.

Standard terms:

- End date in line with the applicable notice period (so the employee remains eligible for WW unemployment benefit).
- Severance equal to the **statutory transition allowance (transitievergoeding)**, sometimes increased.
- 14-day reflection period (BW 7:670b(2)) — the employee can withdraw without giving reasons.
- Recommendation to consult a lawyer; Tulpix contributes up to **€1,500 + VAT** to legal fees.
- Final salary, holiday pay, and unused vacation paid out within the next regular payroll cycle.
- Mutual non-disparagement and confidentiality clause.
- Standard release of claims, with explicit carve-outs for pension, mandatory insurance, and statutory rights.

## 6. Dismissal by Tulpix

### 6.1 Grounds

Dismissal in the Netherlands requires a **redelijke grond** (reasonable ground) per BW 7:669(3):

| Code | Ground | Route |
|------|--------|-------|
| a | Economic reasons | UWV |
| b | Long-term sickness (>104 weeks) | UWV |
| c | Frequent sickness with disproportionate impact | Kantonrechter |
| d | Underperformance | Kantonrechter |
| e | Culpable conduct | Kantonrechter |
| f | Conscientious objection | Kantonrechter |
| g | Disturbed working relationship | Kantonrechter |
| h | Other circumstances | Kantonrechter |
| i | Cumulation ground (combined a–h) | Kantonrechter |

Tulpix never uses the cumulation ground (i) without explicit Legal sign-off.

### 6.2 Performance dismissals (ground d)

Required steps:

1. Documented performance concerns in writing.
2. A formal **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** of at least 8 weeks with clear, measurable objectives.
3. Regular check-ins (weekly) and written feedback.
4. Realistic support: training, coaching, role adjustments where possible.
5. Consideration of **redeployment** within Tulpix (herplaatsingsplicht).
6. Only after these steps — and only if the PIP is genuinely failed — can dismissal be requested at the Kantonrechter.

Skipping any of these steps almost always results in PIP being deemed insufficient by the court.

### 6.3 Reorganization (ground a)

For collective redundancies (>20 employees in 30 days within one work area), the **Wet melding collectief ontslag (WMCO)** applies:

- Notify the trade unions and the UWV.
- Consult the Works Council under WOR art. 25.
- Mandatory waiting period of 1 month before dismissals can take effect.
- Apply the **afspiegelingsbeginsel** (mirror principle) for selecting employees within the same job category.

## 7. Severance (transitievergoeding)

Employees with any length of service are entitled to a transition allowance when:

- Employment is ended by the employer; or
- Employment is not renewed at the end of a fixed-term contract by the employer; or
- Employment ends by mutual consent in lieu of dismissal.

### 7.1 Calculation

- **1/3 of monthly salary per year of service**, pro-rated for partial years.
- Capped in 2026 at **€98,000** or 1 year's salary, whichever is higher.
- "Salary" includes base + holiday allowance + structural bonuses.

Example: 7 years of service at €5,000/month → 7 × (5,000/3) = **€11,667**.

### 7.2 Tulpix top-up

For redundancies (ground a), Tulpix typically pays **150% of the statutory transition allowance**, subject to Works Council consultation.

## 8. Summary dismissal (ontslag op staande voet)

Reserved for **dringende redenen** (urgent and grave reasons) such as:

- Theft or fraud.
- Serious harassment or violence.
- Gross insubordination.
- Willful breach of confidentiality with material harm.

Requirements:

- The employer must act **immediately** upon discovery.
- The grounds must be communicated in writing within hours.
- Legal must approve before notice is given.

This route is rare and high-risk; the bar at court is very high.

## 9. Offboarding checklist

| Owner | Task |
|-------|------|
| Employee | Submit resignation / sign VSO |
| Manager | Communicate to team within 5 days |
| Manager | Identify handover plan |
| People Ops | Final-day calculation, transition allowance estimate |
| People Ops | Schedule exit interview (optional, voluntary) |
| Payroll | Calculate final salary, vacation payout, holiday allowance |
| IT | Disable accounts at 17:00 on last working day |
| IT | Collect laptop and badge |
| Finance | Settle outstanding expenses within 30 days |
| Pension | Notify pension fund of end date |
| Manager | Reference letter on request (factual, dates + role) |

### 9.1 Exit interview

Conducted by People Ops, voluntary, fully anonymized in aggregate reporting. We genuinely use this feedback.

### 9.2 Reference letter (getuigschrift)

Under BW 7:656, employees have a right to a written reference upon request. Tulpix's standard letter includes:

- Dates of employment.
- Roles held.
- A factual description of duties.
- On request: an evaluation (we provide this if it is positive or neutral).

## 10. Post-employment obligations

### 10.1 Confidentiality

The confidentiality clause survives termination indefinitely.

### 10.2 Non-compete and non-solicitation

- Non-compete clauses (concurrentiebeding) must be in writing, with a clear scope and geography. Per the **2025 Wet modernisering concurrentiebeding**, they require a written justification of the protected business interest and a maximum duration of **12 months**.
- Tulpix uses non-compete clauses sparingly: only for senior leadership and a small number of specialist roles.
- Non-solicitation of employees and customers: 12 months by default.

### 10.3 Return of property

All Tulpix property (laptop, badge, phone, documents) must be returned by the last working day. Failure to return may result in deduction from final salary, with notice.

## 11. Retirement

- State pension age (AOW) in 2026 is **67 years and 3 months**.
- Tulpix employment contracts end automatically at AOW age unless extended in writing.
- A retirement send-off is organized by People Ops in coordination with the team.
- Retirees are invited to the annual alumni event.

## 12. Death of an employee

In the unfortunate event of an employee's death:

- Salary is paid until the end of the month of death.
- A **one-time payment of 1 month's salary** is made to the surviving partner or heirs, in addition to any pension fund death benefit.
- People Ops liaises with the family for practical matters (final pay, pension, personal items).
- A bereavement counsellor is offered to the team.

## 13. Disputes

Any dispute arising from termination should first be raised with People Ops. If unresolved:

- Mediation via a registered MfN mediator (Tulpix pays 50% of the cost up to €2,500).
- Subkantonrechter (subdistrict court) for legal proceedings.
