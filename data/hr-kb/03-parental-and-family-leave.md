# Parental, Maternity & Family Leave Policy

**Document ID:** HR-200
**Version:** 2.4
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees in the EU

---

## 1. Overview

Tulpix complies with the EU Work-Life Balance Directive (2019/1158) and Dutch law on work and care (Wet arbeid en zorg, WAZO). This policy describes the leave types available to expecting parents, new parents, and employees caring for family members.

All leave is paid through Dutch UWV (the social security agency) where statutory, with Tulpix topping up to **100% of regular salary** as described below.

## 2. Pregnancy and maternity leave (zwangerschaps- en bevallingsverlof)

### 2.1 Entitlement

Pregnant employees are entitled to **at least 16 weeks of paid leave**, split as follows:

- **Pregnancy leave:** 4 to 6 weeks **before** the expected due date.
- **Maternity leave:** 10 to 12 weeks **after** birth.

The total is always at least 16 weeks, regardless of the actual birth date. If the baby is born later than expected, the post-birth period is extended so the total leave is never reduced.

### 2.2 Multiple births

For twins, triplets, or more, total leave is extended to **20 weeks**, with pregnancy leave starting 8–10 weeks before the due date.

### 2.3 Premature or late births

- If the baby is born more than 6 weeks before the due date, unused pre-birth leave is **added to** post-birth leave.
- Hospitalization of the newborn beyond 7 days extends maternity leave by the number of additional hospital days, up to a maximum of 10 weeks extra.

### 2.4 Pay

- UWV pays a maternity benefit equal to 100% of the daily wage, capped at the **maximum daily wage** (in 2026: €283.16/day).
- Tulpix tops up any difference between the UWV cap and the employee's regular salary, so total compensation is **100% of regular salary** for the full leave period.

### 2.5 Notification

Notify HR at least **3 weeks** before pregnancy leave is to start. Provide a statement from your midwife or doctor with the expected due date.

## 3. Partner / paternity leave (geboorteverlof + aanvullend geboorteverlof)

### 3.1 Birth leave (1 week, fully paid by Tulpix)

The non-birthing partner is entitled to **1 week (5 working days) of fully paid leave** to be taken within **4 weeks** of the birth. Pay is 100% of salary, paid by Tulpix.

### 3.2 Additional partner leave (up to 5 weeks)

Within **6 months of the birth**, the partner may take an additional **5 weeks of partner leave**. UWV pays 70% of the daily wage (capped at the max daily wage). **Tulpix tops up to 100% of regular salary.**

### 3.3 Eligibility

Available to the registered partner of the birthing parent, including same-sex partners and adoptive partners. Must be the legal parent or recognized partner of the child.

## 4. Parental leave (ouderschapsverlof)

### 4.1 Entitlement

Each parent is entitled to **26 times the weekly working hours** of parental leave per child, until the child turns 8 years old. For a 40-hour-per-week employee, this is **1,040 hours** (≈ 26 weeks of full-time absence).

### 4.2 Paid portion

The first **9 weeks** of parental leave (per parent, per child) are paid by UWV at **70% of the daily wage** (capped at max daily wage), provided the leave is taken within **the first year** after birth or adoption.

Tulpix supplements this with an additional 15% top-up, bringing the paid portion to **85% of regular salary** for those 9 weeks.

The remaining **17 weeks (for a full-timer) are unpaid**, but pension contributions continue at the full rate paid by Tulpix.

### 4.3 Scheduling

Parental leave can be taken:
- Full-time blocks
- Part-time (e.g., reducing weekly hours by 20%)
- Spread across multiple years until the child turns 8

Submit a request via Workday at least **2 months in advance**. Tulpix may delay (but not refuse) a requested schedule for compelling business reasons, in consultation with the employee.

## 5. Adoption and foster care leave

Employees adopting a child or taking in a long-term foster child are entitled to **6 weeks of paid leave**, paid by UWV at 100% of the daily wage (capped). Tulpix tops up to 100% of regular salary.

The leave can start 4 weeks before the child arrives and must be taken within **26 weeks** of arrival. Both partners are entitled.

## 6. Short-term care leave (kortdurend zorgverlof)

For necessary care of a sick family member, employees may take up to **2 weeks per 12-month period** (calculated as twice the weekly working hours).

- Pay: **70% of regular salary**, paid by Tulpix (with a floor of the statutory minimum wage).
- Eligible relatives: spouse/partner, children (incl. step- and foster), parents, grandparents, grandchildren, siblings, housemates, and others with whom the employee has a social relationship.

## 7. Long-term care leave (langdurend zorgverlof)

For a family member who is seriously ill and life-threatening, employees may take up to **6 times the weekly working hours per 12-month period** (e.g., 240 hours for a full-timer).

- This is **unpaid** under Dutch law.
- Tulpix offers an optional top-up of **30% of regular salary** during the first 4 weeks, applied for via HR on a case-by-case basis.

## 8. Lactation breaks

For up to **9 months after birth**, breastfeeding employees may take paid breaks to nurse or express milk, up to **25% of working time per shift**. Tulpix provides a private lactation room at every office (see facilities map on the intranet).

## 9. Returning to work

- Employees retain the right to return to the same or an equivalent role.
- Within 1 year of returning from parental or maternity leave, employees may request changes to their working hours under the Wet flexibel werken (Flexible Working Act). Tulpix will respond in writing within 1 month.
- All return-to-work conversations are scheduled by the line manager 2 weeks before the planned return date.

## 10. Subsidiary-specific addenda

- **Germany (Tulpix GmbH):** Mutterschutz applies (6 weeks pre-birth + 8 weeks post-birth, fully paid). Elternzeit replaces parental leave provisions in this policy.
- **France (Tulpix SAS):** Congé maternité (16 weeks for first/second child, 26 weeks from third). Congé paternité (28 days, of which 7 mandatory).
- **Spain (Tulpix Iberia S.L.):** 16 weeks fully paid for each parent, including non-birthing parent.
- **Ireland (Tulpix Ireland Ltd.):** Maternity Benefit (26 weeks paid + 16 weeks unpaid). Paternity Benefit (2 weeks). Parent's Leave (9 weeks paid).

Local addenda are maintained on the intranet under *People > Local Policies*.
