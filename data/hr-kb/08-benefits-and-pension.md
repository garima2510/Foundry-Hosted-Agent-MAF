## 1. Overview

Tulpix offers a comprehensive benefits package built around three pillars:

1. **Financial security** — pension, life insurance, disability cover.
2. **Health & wellbeing** — health insurance contribution, mental health support, fitness allowance.
3. **Lifestyle & family** — commuting, childcare, learning, mobility, sustainability.

Most benefits are administered through the Tulpix Benefits Portal at https://benefits.tulpix.example (single sign-on via Workday).

## 2. Pension (Netherlands)

### 2.1 Provider

Tulpix's pension scheme is administered by **PensioenFonds Tulpix** (a Premium Pension Institution — PPI), in line with the Dutch Pensioenwet and the Wet toekomst pensioenen (WTP).

### 2.2 Type of scheme

- **Defined contribution (premieovereenkomst)** with age-independent flat-rate contribution, in line with WTP requirements (transition complete by 1 January 2027 across the industry; Tulpix transitioned in 2025).
- **Total contribution: 22% of pension base.**
  - Tulpix pays **17%** (employer share).
  - Employee pays **5%** (deducted pre-tax from gross salary).

### 2.3 Pension base

Pension base = annual gross salary − the AOW franchise (€17,545 in 2026).

Example: an employee earning €60,000 gross has a pension base of €60,000 − €17,545 = €42,455.

### 2.4 Voluntary additions

Employees may contribute extra (bijspaarmodule) up to the **fiscal maximum** under Dutch tax law. Contact rewards@tulpix.example for advice.

### 2.5 Vesting and transfer

- All contributions vest immediately. There is no waiting period.
- Upon leaving Tulpix, employees can transfer the accrued capital (waardeoverdracht) to their next employer's scheme or leave it with PensioenFonds Tulpix.

### 2.6 Subsidiary pensions

- **Germany:** Direktversicherung via Allianz Leben. Tulpix matches employee contributions up to 4% of salary.
- **France:** PER d'entreprise collectif via AXA. Tulpix matches up to 5%.
- **Spain:** Plan de pensiones de empleo via VidaCaixa. Tulpix contributes 4%, employee may contribute up to 4%.
- **Ireland:** Master Trust DC scheme via Irish Life. Tulpix matches employee contributions up to 7%.

## 3. Health insurance

### 3.1 Netherlands

In the Netherlands, basic health insurance (Zorgverzekering) is mandatory and individually purchased. Tulpix:

- Pays the statutory **employer health insurance contribution (ZVW werkgeversheffing)** of 6.51% over salary up to €71,628 in 2026 — required by law.
- Reimburses **€100/month** towards the cost of supplementary health insurance (aanvullende verzekering), processed via payroll.

### 3.2 Other countries

- **Germany:** Statutory or private; employer pays 50% of statutory contribution, capped.
- **France:** Mandatory mutuelle via Alan, with Tulpix paying **70%** of the family premium.
- **Spain:** Optional private health via Sanitas; Tulpix pays full cost for employee, 50% for partner & dependants.
- **Ireland:** Optional private cover via VHI Healthcare. Tulpix contributes €1,800/year per employee.

## 4. Disability and life insurance

- **WIA-aanvullingsverzekering (NL):** Tulpix carries collective WGA top-up cover, ensuring at least 70% of last salary in case of partial disability beyond 2 years (see `04-sick-leave-policy.md` section 8).
- **Group life insurance:** Lump sum equivalent to **2 × annual gross salary**, paid to designated beneficiary in case of death during employment.
- **ANW-hiaatverzekering:** Optional supplementary partner/orphan insurance, employee-funded via payroll (NL only).

## 5. Mental health and wellbeing

- **OpenUp** EAP (psychologists, mindfulness, financial coaching) — 24/7, free for employees and household members, unlimited sessions.
- **Headspace** subscription — free for all employees.
- **Wellbeing budget:** €600/year per employee, claimable via the Benefits Portal for fitness, gym, sports clubs, ergonomic equipment, mindfulness apps, or wellness retreats.
- **Annual flu shot** — free at the Amsterdam HQ in October each year.

## 6. Family

- **Childcare contribution:** Tulpix reimburses up to **€350/month per child** (under 13) for registered childcare (kinderopvang), in addition to the Dutch kinderopvangtoeslag (state subsidy). Submit invoices monthly via the Benefits Portal.
- **Adoption support:** €5,000 one-time payment for legal/agency fees per adopted child.
- **Fertility support:** €10,000 lifetime cap towards IVF, IUI, or related treatments not covered by insurance.

## 7. Commuting

### 7.1 Netherlands

- **NS-Business Card** — fully reimbursed first-class public transit between home and office, plus business travel.
- **Bicycle plan** (fietsplan / bedrijfsfiets) — Tulpix provides a company bicycle (lease) up to €2,500, fully tax-optimized; employee pays a small monthly bijtelling. Refresh every 4 years.
- **Mileage** — €0.23/km tax-free for unavoidable car commuting (max 20,000 km/year).

### 7.2 Other countries

- **Germany:** Deutschland-Ticket fully covered. JobRad bicycle leasing.
- **France:** Full Navigo / public transport reimbursement (50% legal + 25% Tulpix top-up). Forfait mobilités durables €700/year.
- **Spain:** Tulpix pays public transport season pass; Bicimad subscription covered in Madrid.
- **Ireland:** TaxSaver commuter ticket scheme; Bike to Work scheme.

## 8. Sustainability and mobility

- **Lease car (NL):** Available for L5+ employees. Only **fully electric** vehicles are permitted from 2026 onward. Bijtelling 17% (electric) up to €30,000 cap.
- **EV home charger:** €1,500 contribution towards installation if no lease car is taken (NL).
- **Carbon offset:** Tulpix offsets all flights booked through the corporate travel system.

## 9. Time-based benefits

- **Birthday day off:** Take the working day closest to your birthday off, paid.

## 10. Discounts

- 30% discount on Tulpix products (employee + 5 family members).
- Corporate discounts portal (Benefits Hub) with offers from electronics, travel, eyewear, and more.

## 11. Tax treatment (Netherlands)

Tulpix uses the Dutch **werkkostenregeling (WKR)** to provide tax-efficient benefits:

- The free space (vrije ruimte) in 2026 is 1.92% of total taxable wages up to €400,000 and 1.18% above.
- Items provided under the targeted exemptions (gerichte vrijstellingen) — e.g., mobile phone for work, home internet for work — do not consume free space.
- The home office allowance (€2.40/day), commuting reimbursement (€0.23/km), and bicycle plan are designed to fit within statutory exemptions.

## 12. Changing your benefits

- Most benefits can be changed annually during **open enrollment in November**.
- Life events (marriage, birth, divorce, partner's job change) trigger a 60-day window to make changes outside open enrollment.
- Submit changes via the Benefits Portal. Pension changes are processed within 30 days; insurance changes within 60 days.
