# Leave & Vacation Policy

**Document ID:** HR-100
**Version:** 3.1
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees in the EU

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy describes the types of paid and unpaid leave available to Tulpix employees, in line with Dutch law (Wet arbeid en zorg, Burgerlijk Wetboek Boek 7) and the EU Working Time Directive (2003/88/EC).

## 2. Statutory annual leave

### 2.1 Entitlement

- Full-time employees (40 hours/week) are entitled to **25 paid vacation days** per calendar year.
  - 20 of these are statutory days under Dutch law (4 × the weekly working hours).
  - 5 are **additional contractual days** granted by Tulpix.
- Part-time employees accrue vacation pro-rata to their contracted hours. Example: a 24-hour-per-week employee accrues 25 × (24 / 40) = **15 days** per year.
- New hires accrue vacation from their first day of employment, pro-rated for the remainder of the calendar year.

### 2.2 Holiday allowance (vakantiegeld)

All Dutch employees receive a statutory holiday allowance of **8% of gross annual salary**, paid in a single lump sum in **May** of each year. This is in addition to monthly salary.

For employees in other EU countries, the local equivalent (e.g., 13th-month pay in Germany under collective bargaining, where applicable) is governed by the local addendum to this policy.

### 2.3 Expiry rules

- **Statutory days (20)** expire **6 months after the end of the year in which they were accrued** — i.e., days accrued in 2026 expire on **1 July 2027** unless the employee was unable to take them due to illness or employer obstruction.
- **Contractual days (5)** expire **5 years after the end of the accrual year**, in line with Article 7:642 BW.
- Tulpix encourages employees to take all leave within the calendar year.

### 2.4 Booking and approval

1. Submit leave requests via Workday at least **2 weeks** in advance for absences of 3 days or more.
2. Absences of more than **2 consecutive weeks** require approval at least **6 weeks** in advance.
3. Manager approval is required for all leave. Approval may be denied for urgent business reasons but cannot unreasonably restrict the employee's right to take statutory leave.
4. If denied, the manager must propose an alternative date within 14 days.

## 3. Public holidays

Tulpix observes the public holidays of the country in which the employee is contracted. See `02-public-holidays.csv` for the full schedule.

If a public holiday falls on a Saturday or Sunday, **no compensatory day is given** in the Netherlands (this is standard Dutch practice). Employees in Germany, France, Spain, and Ireland follow local rules.

## 4. Special paid leave (kort verzuim / calamiteitenverlof)

Paid short-term leave is granted for the following events. Notify your manager as soon as possible and log the absence in Workday.

| Event | Paid days |
|-------|-----------|
| Marriage or registered partnership of the employee | 2 days |
| Marriage of a child, parent, or sibling | 1 day |
| Birth of a child (for the non-birthing partner — see also `03-parental-and-family-leave.md`) | 1 day on day of birth |
| Death of a spouse, partner, or child living at home | 4 days |
| Death of a parent, sibling, or child not living at home | 2 days, plus the day of the funeral |
| Death of a grandparent, grandchild, or in-law | 1 day, plus the day of the funeral |
| Moving house (max once per year) | 1 day |
| Doctor, dentist, or specialist appointment that cannot be scheduled outside working hours | Time needed |
| Civic duties (e.g., jury duty, voting in remote regions) | Time needed |

## 5. Unpaid leave (onbetaald verlof)

Employees with at least 12 months of service may request up to **6 months of unpaid leave** in a rolling 5-year period. Requests must be submitted at least 3 months in advance and require approval from the manager and HR. Approval is at Tulpix's discretion and depends on operational continuity.

During unpaid leave:
- Pension contributions pause (employee may opt to continue with full self-funding).
- Health insurance remains the employee's responsibility under the Dutch Zorgverzekeringswet.
- Vacation accrual pauses.

## 6. Sabbatical

After **5 years of continuous service**, employees may apply for a sabbatical of **2 to 6 months**. Sabbaticals are unpaid by default but employees may use accrued vacation days to partially fund the period. See your HR business partner for details.

## 7. Religious and cultural observances

Tulpix supports employees observing religious or cultural days not included in the public holiday calendar. Up to **2 days per calendar year** may be substituted for public holidays of equivalent length, subject to manager approval and at least 4 weeks' notice.

## 8. Carry-over and payout

- Carry-over of unused vacation is automatic up to the expiry limits in section 2.3.
- **Payout of unused vacation is only permitted upon termination of employment**, calculated at the employee's final gross daily rate. Dutch law prohibits payout of statutory leave during active employment (Art. 7:640 BW).

## 9. Leave during sick leave

Employees on sick leave continue to accrue vacation days at the full statutory rate. They may also take vacation while sick, with the agreement of the company doctor (bedrijfsarts) and manager — this temporarily lifts reintegration obligations during the vacation period.
