# Working Hours, Overtime & Rest Periods

**Document ID:** HR-300
**Version:** 2.2
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Applies to:** All Tulpix employees in the EU

---

## 1. Standard working hours

- **Full-time:** 40 hours per week, typically Monday to Friday between 08:00 and 18:00 local time.
- **Standard workday:** 8 hours, plus a minimum 30-minute unpaid break.
- **Part-time:** any agreed weekly hours below 40, pro-rating salary, vacation, and benefits.

Tulpix offers **flexible start and end times**: employees may start any time between 07:00 and 10:00, provided they are available during the **core hours of 10:00–16:00 local time** for collaboration.

## 2. EU Working Time Directive (2003/88/EC) compliance

Tulpix observes the maximum and minimum limits set by the directive, as transposed into the Dutch Arbeidstijdenwet:

| Limit | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Maximum average weekly hours (over 16 weeks) | 48 hours, including overtime |
| Maximum hours per shift | 12 hours |
| Maximum hours per week (peak) | 60 hours |
| Daily rest period | 11 consecutive hours between shifts |
| Weekly rest period | 36 consecutive hours per 7 days, or 72 hours per 14 days |
| Minimum break (shift > 5.5 hours) | 30 minutes |
| Minimum break (shift > 10 hours) | 45 minutes (may be split into 15-minute blocks) |
| Night work (shifts ending after 02:00) | Avg max 40 hours/week over 16 weeks |
| Sundays worked per year | Max 13, unless contractually agreed otherwise |

## 3. Overtime

### 3.1 General principle

Tulpix does not expect routine overtime. Sustainable working hours are central to our culture.

### 3.2 When overtime is required

Where overtime is necessary (e.g., production incidents, customer escalations, release windows), the following rules apply:

- Pre-approved by the line manager.
- Logged in Workday under *Time > Overtime* with reason code.
- Compensated either as **time off in lieu (TOIL) at 1:1**, or — for employees in operations and 24/7 on-call functions — as paid overtime at:
  - **125%** of base hourly rate on weekdays
  - **150%** on Saturdays
  - **200%** on Sundays and public holidays

### 3.3 Senior employees

Employees in roles classified as **"Senior" (level S5+)** have overtime expectations included in their base salary. They do not receive additional overtime pay but may take TOIL informally with manager agreement.

## 4. On-call duty (consignering)

For roles requiring on-call coverage:

- On-call shifts are scheduled at least 4 weeks in advance.
- An on-call allowance of **€2.50 per on-call hour outside normal working hours** is paid.
- Active work during on-call is paid at the overtime rates above.
- A maximum of **104 on-call shifts per 12-month period** per employee.
- Mandatory rest after a callout exceeding 2 hours: 8 hours before next shift.

## 5. Time tracking

- All employees log working time in Workday weekly. Submission is due **by Monday 12:00 local time** for the previous week.
- Hourly and project-based employees log per project for billing and resource planning.
- Salaried employees log only deviations from the standard (e.g., overtime, sick, leave) plus weekly project allocation.

This complies with the EU Court of Justice ruling C-55/18 (CCOO v. Deutsche Bank) requiring objective, reliable, and accessible time-tracking systems.

## 6. Rest, breaks, and the right to disconnect

### 6.1 Right to disconnect

Tulpix recognizes the **right to disconnect** outside agreed working hours. Employees are not required to:

- Read or respond to work emails, messages, or calls outside their scheduled hours.
- Be available during agreed time off, vacation, or sick leave.

Managers must respect this right and lead by example. Repeated violations are addressed under section 9 of the Code of Conduct.

### 6.2 Vacation handover

Before any vacation of 5+ working days, employees are expected to:

1. Set an out-of-office reply with a colleague's contact.
2. Hand over urgent matters via a documented checklist.
3. Disable work notifications on personal devices.

## 7. Reduced hours and flexible work requests

Under the Dutch Wet flexibel werken (Flexible Working Act), employees with at least **26 weeks** of service may request:

- Adjusted weekly hours (more or fewer)
- Adjusted working pattern (e.g., compressed week, 4-day week)
- Adjusted work location (see also `06-remote-and-hybrid-work.md`)

Tulpix will respond in writing within **1 month**. Refusal requires substantial business justification (e.g., demonstrable financial harm, scheduling conflicts that cannot be resolved).

A request may not be made more often than **once every 12 months**, unless there is a substantial change in circumstances.

## 8. Night work and shift work

Roles routinely involving night work (shifts ending after 02:00 or starting before 06:00) are:

- Limited to specified operations and security positions.
- Subject to a free medical examination before starting and every 2 years thereafter.
- Compensated with a night-shift allowance of **20% of base hourly rate** for hours worked between 21:00 and 07:00.
- Limited to **maximum 7 consecutive nights**, followed by at least 46 hours of rest.

## 9. Sunday work

Sunday work is voluntary unless explicitly agreed in the employment contract or required by the role (e.g., 24/7 operations). Refusal is not grounds for disciplinary action.

If Sunday work is required:
- Notice must be given at least **2 weeks** in advance.
- Compensation is 200% of hourly rate or equivalent TOIL at 1:2.

## 10. Recording and audits

The People team conducts a quarterly review of time records. Patterns of excessive hours (>48 average over 16 weeks) trigger:

1. A confidential conversation with the employee.
2. A workload review with the manager.
3. If systemic, a team-level intervention (additional headcount, scope reduction, process review).

## 11. Subsidiary specifics

- **Germany:** Arbeitszeitgesetz max 8 hours/day (extendable to 10 with compensation). Sunday work generally prohibited.
- **France:** 35-hour legal working week. Cadres autonomes may have forfait-jours arrangements (max 218 days/year).
- **Spain:** 40-hour week. Time tracking under Real Decreto-ley 8/2019.
- **Ireland:** Organisation of Working Time Act 1997. Average 48-hour max over 4 months.
